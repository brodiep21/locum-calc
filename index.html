Methodology & Key Assumptions

  • Federal Tax Brackets: Uses 2026 brackets (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%) per IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-32 and OBBBA permanent extension. Taxable income = AGI minus the greater of standard or itemized deductions.
  • 2026 Standard Deductions: Single $16,100 · Married Filing Jointly $32,200 · Head of Household $24,150.
  • Self-Employment Tax: 15.3% on 92.35% of net SE income (Social Security on first $184,500 for 2026, Medicare uncapped). The deductible half of SE tax is accounted for.
  • S-Corp Savings: Only the "reasonable salary" owes FICA (7.65% employee + 7.65% employer). Distributions above salary avoid SE tax. The employer-side FICA is a deductible business expense.
  • Solo 401k — Employee Deferral: This is money YOU put in, just like contributing to a regular 401k at a job. Hard-capped at $24,500/yr (under 50) for 2026. Reduces your taxable income dollar for dollar.
  • Solo 401k — Employer Profit-Sharing: Since you own the business, your business can ALSO contribute on top of the employee deferral. Limited to 25% of your S-Corp W-2 salary, or ~20% of sole prop adjusted net. Combined with employee deferral, the total cap is $72,000 ($80,000 if 50+) for 2026. The field auto-caps to the allowed amount and shows the max under the input.
  • Profit-Sharing reduces distribution & take-home: The employer contribution is a deductible business expense — it lowers your S-Corp K-1 distribution (and taxable pass-through income) and, since it's money locked into retirement, it reduces spendable take-home. Raising it shifts dollars from taxable cash into tax-sheltered savings.
  • Take-Home definition: Spendable cash only = taxable gross − all taxes − self-funded expenses − ALL retirement contributions (employee + employer) + non-taxable reimbursements. Retirement wealth is reported separately in the Bottom Line.
  • W-2 401k: Your chosen contribution % applied to base salary (capped at $24,500 for 2026), plus employer match. Overtime and bonuses not included in 401k calculation.
  • Overtime Pay (W-2): Added to gross income, subject to all taxes. Not included in 401k contribution base (most employer plans use base salary only).
  • Travel Reimbursement (Locum): Treated as accountable plan reimbursement — non-taxable, not included in gross income. Directly increases your take-home without tax impact.
  • Sign-On / Relocation Bonuses (Locum): Treated as taxable income added to gross revenue.
  • QBI Deduction: Not modeled (phases out above $394,600 MFJ for specified service trades like medicine).
  • Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% on earnings above $200k (single) / $250k (MFJ).
  • PTO Value: W-2 PTO valued at daily rate (salary ÷ 260 working days). Locum unpaid days off reduce gross income instead.
  • Write-offs (Locum): Business expenses deducted before SE tax and income tax. Includes health, malpractice, disability, travel, lodging, licensing, CME, home office, phone, equipment.
  • Colorado: Default state rate 4.4% flat. Adjust for your state.
